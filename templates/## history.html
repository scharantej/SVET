History of Siberia

Overview

Siberia has a long and rich history, dating back to the Paleolithic era. The region has been home to a variety of nomadic and settled peoples, including the Scythians, the Huns, the Mongols, and the Russians.

Timeline

  • 10,000 BC: First evidence of human habitation in Siberia.
  • 8,000 BC: Development of agriculture in Siberia.
  • 3,000 BC: Arrival of the Scythians in Siberia.
  • 1,000 BC: Arrival of the Huns in Siberia.
  • 1206: Genghis Khan conquers Siberia.
  • 1581: The Russian Empire begins its conquest of Siberia.
  • 1648: The Treaty of Nerchinsk establishes the border between Russia and China in Siberia.
  • 1869: The Trans-Siberian Railway is completed.
  • 1917: The Russian Revolution leads to the establishment of the Soviet Union.
  • 1991: The Soviet Union collapses and Siberia becomes part of the Russian Federation.

Important Eras

  • Prehistoric Era: Siberia was inhabited by nomadic tribes who relied on hunting and gathering for survival.
  • Scythian Era: The Scythians were a nomadic people who migrated to Siberia from Central Asia. They were skilled horsemen and archers.
  • Hunnic Era: The Huns were a nomadic people who conquered Siberia in the 4th century AD. They were known for their ferocity and military prowess.
  • Mongol Era: The Mongols conquered Siberia in the 13th century AD. They established a vast empire that stretched from Eastern Europe to China.
  • Russian Era: The Russians began their conquest of Siberia in the 16th century AD. They gradually expanded their territory eastward, eventually reaching the Pacific Ocean.
  • Soviet Era: Siberia was part of the Soviet Union from 1917 to 1991. During this time, the region was heavily industrialized and developed.
  • Post-Soviet Era: After the collapse of the Soviet Union, Siberia became part of the Russian Federation. The region has been facing economic and social challenges since then.
