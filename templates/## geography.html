Geography of Siberia

Overview

Siberia is a vast and diverse region that occupies the northern part of Asia. It is home to a variety of landscapes, climates, and natural resources.

Landscapes

  • Taiga: The taiga is a vast forest that covers much of Siberia. It is home to a variety of trees, including spruce, fir, and larch.
  • Tundra: The tundra is a treeless plain that lies north of the taiga. It is characterized by permafrost, which is a layer of frozen soil that never thaws.
  • Steppe: The steppe is a grassland that lies south of the taiga. It is home to a variety of grasses and flowers.
  • Mountains: Siberia is home to a number of mountain ranges, including the Ural Mountains, the Altai Mountains, and the Verkhoyansk Range.
  • Rivers: Siberia is home to a number of major rivers, including the Ob River, the Yenisei River, and the Lena River.

Natural Resources

  • Gas: Siberia is also home to some of the world's largest gas reserves.
  • Minerals: Siberia is rich in minerals, including gold, silver, copper, and nickel.
  • Timber: Siberia is home to vast forests, which provide a valuable source of timber.
  • Furs: Siberia is home to a variety of animals that produce valuable furs, such as sable, fox, and mink.
